  • 4

  • 5

  • 6

  • 7
    Growth ceiling = organic non-brand acquisition. The fix is content + internal linking to collections. Retention is healthy (returning-customer rate 53%). The new topical-authority blog program, link-built down to the high-demand collection pages, is the highest-leverage move available. Evidence

02

Migration: Before → After

A like-for-like read of the GSC footprint — legacy nationltd.com against current nation.la. To keep the comparison honest, the primary lenses are composition (share of clicks) and average position, both independent of how long each property was active. Absolute click totals follow, with the reporting-window caveat applied so nothing is overstated.

How to read this fairly — methodology The two GSC properties do not cover identical active windows: the legacy domain logged most of its clicks while it was the live site (pre- and at-migration), and the current domain accrues clicks after the redirect. Both exports are also capped at each domain's top 1,000 rows. As a result, raw absolute volume deltas overstate the real change — a metric can fall simply because the legacy property had more active reporting time. The trustworthy signals are share-of-clicks and average position, which do not depend on window length. The composition shift below is therefore the conservative, window-independent proof; absolute figures are shown afterward as directional only.

The big driver of the inflated collections delta is one page. /collections/dresses failed to redirect cleanly at migration: for ~3 months (late May → early Sep 2025) the stale legacy URL kept ranking and absorbed branded query traffic, briefly out-earning the homepage and jumping from its usual ~5–10 clicks/day to ~100/day before Google dropped it (clicks fell to zero after ~5 Sep 2025). The genuine, standing limitation is that non-brand visibility was never fully built (page-3 positions, historically brand-constrained content) — and the current category pages still attract large impression-level demand (dresses: 155K impressions, 1,195 clicks) waiting to be captured. That is a content-and-links opportunity, now greenlit. Evidence

SEO Structural Analysis

Comparing the legacy and current GSC footprints across brand/non-brand queries and page clusters isolates what the migration cost — and, importantly, what it did not. The conclusion points directly to the content roadmap in Section 08.

Credit where due The technical migration landed the hard parts: brand equity, homepage authority and product indexing all transferred. Historically the editorial/content layer that defends non-brand category rankings was proposed and pushed, but kept narrow by brand priorities. That constraint has now lifted — so the gap below is an opportunity that is finally actionable, not a fault line.

Four candidate causes, scored against the evidence.

Primary
Under-built non-brand layer

That is a build opportunity, not breakage — and it explains why retention looks fine while new-customer growth stalls. Evidence

Two effects layered on "Direct"

Why the "direct is collapsing" read needs qualifying.

① Bot / data-center inflation Hypothesis

If a meaningful share of Direct sessions are non-human, they inflate the session denominator and depress apparent engagement — making the human experience look worse than it is. Needs GA4 bot-segmentation to size.

② Attribution re-bucketing Evidence

Post-migration, revenue moved out of None·None (−39%) into None·nation (+76%) — consistent with the same humans being re-labeled as the new domain's self-referral, rather than a pure demand swing.

What we can & cannot claim Reasonably supported: attribution re-bucketing shifted how Direct revenue is labeled (two signals: referrer revenue shift + the post-migration self-referral pattern). Not yet proven: a causal lag between remarketing spend and Direct sessions, and the precise scale of bot inflation. Both need a daily GA4 Direct-session series with bot filtering, aligned to daily remarketing spend. Until then, treat "Direct is collapsing" as a partial measurement effect layered on the real (~30%) volume decline.

07

Strategic Implications

What this means for growth — plainly, with precision retained.

What needs work

  • • Organic non-brand acquisition — category-page authority and rankings for product/category terms.
  • • New-visitor inflow — every paid and organic channel is bringing a smaller share of new visitors (NVP down across Meta, Google, Pinterest, Organic), so the top of the funnel is narrowing — consistent with orders −30%.
  • • Margin pressure — discounts +21% and returns +15% are buying volume rather than acquiring it cheaply.

What is working

  • • Technical migration — brand + homepage equity transferred; product pages grew.
  • • Product-page visibility — recovered and grew (+36% clicks).
  • • Retention & email — RCR 53%; Klaviyo the only channel lifting its share of new visitors.
The reframe This is not "the migration broke everything," and not "direct traffic collapsed." The migration's hard technical work succeeded; the missing piece is the content and internal-linking layer that feeds non-brand category acquisition — historically constrained, now unlocked. Rebuilding it with the topical-authority program re-opens the demand feeder the whole funnel relies on, and eases the margin pressure created by leaning on paid. That is the single highest-leverage move.
